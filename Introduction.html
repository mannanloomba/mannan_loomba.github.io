Noise pollution monitoring system intoduction:

A Noise Pollution Monitoring System (NPMS) is a vital tool in the battle against noise pollution. Noise pollution, originating from sources like traffic, industrial activities, and construction, can have detrimental effects on human health and well-being, making its monitoring and mitigation crucial. An NPMS is designed to continuously assess noise levels, employing an array of sensors to capture real-time data. This system offers real-time monitoring, data analysis, and the ability to issue alerts when noise levels surpass predetermined thresholds. Historical data and visualization tools aid in tracking trends and informing policy decisions. NPMS not only benefits authorities and researchers but also engages the public in noise reduction efforts. It is instrumental in zoning and land use planning, source identification, compliance monitoring, and health impact assessment. By facilitating informed decisions and fostering community involvement, NPMS plays a pivotal role in improving the quality of life in urban areas and promoting sustainable development.
